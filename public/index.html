Getting a loan with bad credit is difficult

It should come as no surprise that banks want to know how likely you are to repay them before giving you a new loan.

One of the most common ways they do it is by ordering your credit score from companies like FICO and VantageScore. These credit-scoring models analyze your creditworthiness based on information in your credit reports, such as loan amounts and payment history.

When you have a low credit score, you'll likely have to pay higher interest rates to offset your high level of default risk, whether you're applying for a car loan, a mortgage, or a credit card account.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How are you able to help me?

    Our clients receive two benefits from joining our program. The first and most important benefit is that we create and send specialized disputes on your behalf that are designed to eliminate specific negative items from your credit report.

  • How long does it take to repair my credit?

    It's a very common question we receive, but unfortunately we do not have a straight answer to it. This is because it is determined by how the companies you owe money to (creditors and collection agencies) handle our dispute.

  • With which companies do you assist in disputing negative information?

    There are three major credit bureaus we dispute: TransUnion, Equifax, and Experian.

  • What is considered a bad credit score?

    Anything below 670 is considered poor or only fair credit.

  • Why do I have three credit scores?

    A credit score is not a single number. Each of the three credit bureaus gives you a customized score based on the information on your credit report, and you also have a FICOÂ® Score

  • How can I get a better credit score?

    Payment history, debt amount, length of credit history, credit mix, and new credit all contribute to your credit score. Paying your bills on time, paying down debt, and maintaining your current accounts can help you raise your credit score.
